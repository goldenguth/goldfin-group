Most owners feel like they have a plan because they have good people around them. A financial advisor, a CPA, an attorney, maybe a banker they've spoken with once or twice. On paper, it looks like a team.

But when you start asking how the business sale connects to the taxes, which connects to the estate plan, which connects to the reinvestment strategy, which connects to what life looks like on the other side of the deal, there's usually a pause. Because no one is coordinating it.

## You don't have a plan. You have parts.

If nobody is waking up in the morning thinking about how all the moving pieces of your exit work together, you don't have a plan. You have parts. That's usually where things start to slip, not all at once, but in ways that become expensive later.

This is the work we do: quarterbacking the whole picture, building the team and calling the plays, so the tax strategy, the legal documents, and the wealth plan all say the same thing.
